## Magi-15 Cost-Aware R Analysis

- User and assistant diagnosed that Magi-15's negative expectancy was likely dominated by transaction costs versus too-small stop distances, not necessarily bad setups.
- Magi-15 uses fixed stake sizing for paper trades (`500` notional), not fixed-risk sizing. Earlier explanation: this came from a simple paper model; it makes R analysis unstable when stop distance is tiny.
- Fee/slippage model observed/explained: fee per side `0.1%`, slippage per side `0.05%`; round-trip fee about `1.00`, slippage about `0.50`, total cost about `1.50` absolute paper currency per trade.
- Current realized R calculation was explained as net R: `risk_amount = stake * stopafstand%`, `gross_pnl = stake * prijsbeweging%`, `net_pnl = gross_pnl - fee - slippage`, `realized_r = net_pnl / risk_amount`.
- Key diagnostic numbers shared with user: overall `Avg Gross R +0.40R`, `Avg Net R -1.52R`, `Avg Cost R -/about 1.92R cost drag`, `Avg Risk Amount EUR 1.34`, average costs `EUR 1.50`. This means average costs exceeded average risk amount.
- Example insight for `volatility_breakout`: winrate around `57.89%`, TP exits 11, SL exits 3, BE exits 5, but average winner only about `+0.08R`, average loser about `-2.46R`, because small stops create tiny EUR risk and costs overwhelm gross gains.
- Per-strategy snapshot before latest filter: `volatility_breakout` 19 trades gross `+0.71R`, net `-0.59R`, avg risk `EUR 1.61`, cost R `1.30`; `breakout_retest` 19 trades gross `+0.47R`, net `-0.98R`, avg risk `EUR 1.50`, cost R `1.45`; `momentum_continuation` 17 trades gross `+0.03R`, net `-3.25R`, avg risk `EUR 0.61`, cost R `3.28`; `scout_touch_rejection` 7 trades gross `+0.29R`, net `-1.16R`, avg risk `EUR 2.59`, cost R `1.45`.
